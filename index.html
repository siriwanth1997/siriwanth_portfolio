Research

MSc Dissertation

MSc Research · Supervised by Dr Helen Fairclough · University of Sheffield

Addressing the Paradox of Robust and Material-Efficient Design

Investigated the fundamental tension between structural robustness and material efficiency in topology-optimised truss systems. Implemented a Python-based adaptive fail-safe optimisation framework to design lightweight steel trusses capable of surviving localised member failure without progressive collapse. Verified results using SAP2000 elastic analysis and developed the Residual Stiffness Index (RSI) as a novel robustness metric. Research directly addresses the challenge of meeting net-zero ambitions while maintaining structural safety, with practical implications for residential and commercial design.
